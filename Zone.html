Excerpt from “Zone” a one- act play

Posted August 24, 2021

Excerpt from “Zone” a one- act play

Lisa: Late 50’s Luke: Lisa’s husband, Late 50’s. Joaquin: Lisa and Luke’s neighbor, Early 70’s.

(Lights change. We can hear the sound of a far away explosion. They all jump up. Silence. They all wait, motionless. Another beat.  Luke lies back down. Joaquin gets up and goes to his corner and works on something with his toolbox. Lisa moves towards the coffee maker.)

LISA


Excuse me, uh, Mr. Romero, would you like something to drink?


JOAQUIN


Joaquin.


LISA


Oh, sure, Joaquin


JOAQUIN


Yes, why not?


LISA


Here you go.


(Hands him some water)


JOAQUIN


Thank you. 


LISA


You’re welcome!


    (A beat)


JOAQUIN


Are the radios working?


LISA


Luke is trying to fix them but we haven’t had much luck yet. They fell in the snow on our way down here. 


JOAQUIN


I heard.


LISA


Yes. It’s unfortunate, but I think we are going to be safe. That’s not what I’m worried about. I just… would like to know how Chicago is doing. My, uh, son is going to school there.


JOAQUIN


Oh. I’m very sorry.


LISA


Don’t be sorry. I’m sure he’s doing just fine.


    (JOAQUIN does not respond. LUKE walks over.)


LUKE


Good morning everyone! It seems to be a little calmer today...only one so far. 


LISA


I wouldn’t say that just yet.


LUKE


Well, I choose to be optimistic. How are we on food?


LISA


We have plenty.

LUKE


We have nothing to worry about then!


LISA (to JOAQUIN)

That’s all he cares about.


LUKE


That’s not true. I also care about beer. Do we have any beer?


LISA


Yes, we do.


LUKE


Then everything is fine under my watch!


    (Luke starts pouring coffee for everyone)


Sleeping on the ground wasn’t so bad.


LISA


Any word on those radios? I still have no service and my power is running out.


LUKE


No luck yet. But I’m sure service will be restored soon enough. They always exaggerate when it comes to these things. I’m sure it’s all miles away, nowhere near us. Remember that time with the forest fires? Forest fires in Wisconsin? Impossible. The reason I moved here in the first place. 


LISA


Yes, you’re right. Not here! Why would they-


LUKE


It’s the same thing this time! We are of no use to them. They’ll be gone soon and we’ll get to call Jeremy who is now probably busy with finals anyways. He probably does not want to be bothered. 


LISA


Yes, you’re probably right.


JOAQUIN



Well, not to be a bearer of bad news, but on TV, I’m afraid-


LUKE


It’s called “panic marketing”


LISA


What?


LUKE


It is very popular now.


LISA


And what on earth is that?


LUKE


They convince you that if you don’t buy this or that, you will be responsible for this mess or missing out in some way. For the world ending and yada yada yada...


JOAQUIN


Well, then how do you explain what happened in New York?


LUKE


Alright, who wants a beer? Come on, Mr. Romero you know you want one, it’ll make you feel better. 


LISA


With your coffee?


LUKE


Why not?


JOAQUIN


I don’t drink.


LUKE


But this may be your last beer before the world ends!


     (a beat).


See what I did there? Panic marketing. I almost had you didn’t I?


(LlSA and LUKE open a beer.)
